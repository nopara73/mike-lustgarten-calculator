Model Notes

Inference uses the exact one-hidden-layer MLP:

score(x) = w₂ᵀ GELU(W₁ ((x-μ)/σ) + b₁) + b₂

Calibration maps score to 10-year aging-related risk and then to a risk-equivalent biological age on the male NHANES reference cohort.

What "Mike-specific" means: this was not trained on Mike alone. It means the model was rebuilt around the exact biomarkers Mike actually has, then calibrated on the male NHANES cohort using that same feature set.
Why that matters: a biological-age model is only as good as the signals it is allowed to see. Instead of squeezing Mike into a generic clock designed for some other panel, this calculator turns his real lab panel into its own measurement instrument. That makes it narrower than a universal clock, but more faithful to the biology his tests can actually express.
The grand but grounded version: this is a bespoke lens on Mike's bloodwork, not a one-size-fits-all age widget. It does not claim to reveal everything about aging, but it does claim something meaningful: given this exact set of markers, it extracts about as much aging-related structure as this dataset can support in a compact model.
